# 🌍 Satellite Methane Detection Problem

## 📌 Overview

Satellite systems are used to detect methane leaks, but there are several problems that reduce their accuracy and effectiveness.

---

## 🚧 Problems

### 1. Cloud & Surface Reflection

* Clouds and surface reflections create confusion in satellite images.
* The system sometimes thinks these reflections are methane leaks.

### 2. Wrong Understanding of Methane Signals

* The system cannot clearly understand what is real methane and what is not.
* This leads to incorrect detection.

### 3. Slow Processing (Delayed Action)

* Data is not processed in real-time.
* It takes **2–3 days** to process the data.
* Because of this delay, action is also delayed.

### 4. No Real-Time Data Processing

* The system does not give instant results.
* This makes it difficult to respond quickly to leaks.

### 5. Cannot Find Exact Source

* The system can detect methane leaks.
* But it cannot tell the **exact location or source** of the leak.

### 6. Action Problem

* If we don’t know the exact source and location,
  → we cannot take proper action.

---

## 🌱 Environmental Impact

* Methane is very dangerous for the climate.

* Over 20 years:

  **1 kg Methane = 80 kg CO₂ impact**

* This makes methane one of the most powerful greenhouse gases.

---

## 💡 Importance of Solving This

* Fixing **large methane leaks** can greatly reduce climate damage.
* Better detection systems can:

  * Improve accuracy
  * Reduce delay
  * Help take faster action

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

To protect the environment, we need a system that:

* Works in **real-time**
* Detects methane **accurately**
* Identifies the **exact source and location**
* Reduces errors caused by clouds and reflections
